# Lineage 2 Interlude party and combat basics.
# Format: [party combat <tokens>] One short true line.

[party combat group lfp lfm] Parties hold up to 9 players; people look for members with LFP/LFM in shout or trade chat.
[party combat xp share level] Party XP is shared and scales with how close members are in level; big level gaps cut everyone's XP.
[party combat tank dd healer buffer] A solid party is a tank to hold aggro, damage dealers, a healer and a buffer.
[party combat soulshot spiritshot shots] Keep soulshots (fighters) or spiritshots (mages) loaded; fighting without shots is far weaker.
[party combat mp potion rest] Mages and healers run out of MP fast; rest to regen or have an Elder recharge them, and carry potions.
[party combat aggro pull] Let the tank pull and build aggro first so mobs don't run at the healer or mages.
[party combat death xp loss] Dying costs XP; a Bishop or other resurrect class can restore most of the lost XP with a res.
[party combat spoil dwarf drops] A Scavenger/Bounty Hunter can Spoil mobs for extra crafting materials before they die.
[party combat raid boss] Raid bosses need a full, buffed party (or more) and give strong drops; people form raids in shout chat.
[party combat kite mage archer] Mages and archers kite, attacking from range and backing off so melee mobs can't close in.
